Ember Denver Topic is Ember.js

10/25/17

People I Met

Jim works for Captain U. Captain U is a company that builds apps for Sports teams and universities. Ember.js is how Captain U uses to create their apps. Jim went to Turing three years ago and loves his new profession in coding.

What I Learned

Meet-up went over GraphQL more so than Ember.js. GraphQL allows to search data you want without getting data you don't want. Facebook started using GraphQL in 2015. One cool thing I learned was about a skeleton-UI. The skeleton.UI creates a phantom (wireframe) image of the app loading to prime the user's experience of the app.

Denver Open Source OS Meetup Topic: Andriod Development

11/09/17

People I Met

James the host worked for a company called eMersive, and he is an Engineer. Triston, an Engineer, with an eagerness to unload tons of information beyond my understanding and Perry also worked at eMersive. Perry worked on cat-log's hunting down errors in code. When I asked Triston what he did at the company he stated, "You can call me a Jr-Engineer that works on anything they give me." Great folks and is shows the passion they have for what they do.

What I Learned

Android is not a program as I first thought. Andriod is an operating system that is a distribution of Linux. Security system that Android uses is SE Linux also known as Security Enhance Linux. eMersive showed how they use Java and C++ and use JIT (just-in-time) that compiles during execution of the two codes to work together. eMersive uses Windows Pc and they all hate it.

Questions I Have

Can Android OS be duel booted onto a Mac laptop? Can you build an application that can be served on both Android and Mac?
